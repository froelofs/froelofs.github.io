Floris Roelofsen

Associate Professor · Institute for Logic, Language, and Computation · University of Amsterdam

The interpretation of linguistic expressions has been investigated in several scientific disciplines, ranging from linguistics and philosophy to logic and cognitive science, leading to many applications in computer science and education. At the heart of this enterprise is a simple but powerful notion of meaning as truth-conditional content: you know what a sentence means if you can tell when it's true and when it's false. Despite its merits, however, this conception of meaning has a fundamental limitation: while suitable for statements, it does not straightforwardly apply to other sentence types, like questions, which are not true or false.

The main aim of my work over the last ten years has been to overcome this limitation. In collaboration with several colleagues, I have developed a new semantic framework called inquisitive semantics, which is based on a more general notion of meaning and opens up new horizons for all disciplines concerned with linguistic interpretation. We are currently exploring these horizons in linguistics, philosophical logic, and cognitive science, and are also looking toward applications in computer science.

My current work is supported by a VIDI grant from the Netherlands Organization for Scientific Research (2015-2020) and a Starting Grant from the European Research Council (2016-2021).
